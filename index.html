About

I've spent the last decade at Intuit building things that didn't have templates. TurboTax Full Service didn't exist when I joined. I helped design it from scratch alongside tax experts, service designers, operations, and delivery teams. I then ran design strategy for TurboTax Business, and I now own top-of-funnel acquisition for Assisted Tax: the surfaces that determine whether the right customer takes the first step toward filing with an expert.

I work at the intersection of design, strategy, and data, not as separate modes I shift between, but as concurrent tracks. I build frameworks that hold up under scrutiny, run controlled experiments, and push back when the data is being interpreted wrong. I can design the pixel, read the experiment result, and explain why a finding from one surface doesn't transfer to another.

AI is part of how I work every day. I use it to pressure-test ideas, accelerate synthesis, and sharpen my thinking before it goes into a room. Designers who aren't using these tools yet are leaving capability on the table.

Approach

Framing

The real problem is rarely the stated one.

I work upstream of the brief. In Business Tax, the program was optimizing starts. I named the broken architecture causing them. A problem with no owner, no PRD, no prior framing. That reframe changed what got built, not just how it looked.
Systems

Build what the next ten screens inherit from.

Pattern libraries, diagnostics, test cascades designed to compound. I don't design a screen, I build the structure that makes the next ten faster and more coherent. Vision and execution in parallel, not in sequence.
Fluency

Know what the data is actually saying.

I track business outcomes, not just UX metrics. I read experiment results and know when a finding is real, when it transfers to another surface, and when a number is being asked to mean something it doesn't.
